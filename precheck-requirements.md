# Pre-Hire Check Requirements (from user)

## Three Phases

### Phase 1: Structural & Fluid Checks (Engine OFF)
The operator must physically walk around the machine to check for wear, structural damage, and fluid levels before starting the ignition.

1. **Logbook & Compliance Plates**: Confirm the machine serial number matches, safety decals are legible, and the previous day's defects were repaired.
2. **Fluid Levels & Leaks**: Check engine oil, hydraulic oil, and coolant levels. Inspect the ground underneath for active puddles or drips.
3. **Undercarriage / Tracks**: Inspect track tension, rollers, idlers, sprockets, and final drives for excessive wear, damage, or packed mud/debris.
4. **Boom, Arm & Bucket**: Check for cracked welds, structural stress lines, and ensure all pins and retainers are locked tight.
5. **Hydraulics**: Visually inspect all hoses, cylinders, and couplings for fraying, rubbing, or weeping oil.
6. **Battery & Isolator**: Verify the battery terminals are clean and tight, and that the physical battery isolator switch functions.

### Phase 2: Cabin & Systems Checks (Engine ON)
Once inside the cab, the operator turns on the ignition to verify internal electronics, vital safety frameworks, and visibility aids.

1. **Structure Protection (ROPS/FOPS)**: Ensure Roll-Over and Falling-Object protective bars are structurally sound with no unapproved modifications or welds.
2. **Seat & Seatbelt**: The seatbelt must be present, free of frays, and the latch mechanism must lock firmly.
3. **Visibility**: Check that all cabin windows are clean, mirrors are intact and adjusted, and camera feeds (if equipped) display clearly.
4. **Gauges & Warning Lights**: Ensure the dashboard gauges read correctly (fuel/temperature) and no critical fault warning lights remain on.
5. **Safety Equipment**: Confirm an in-date, fully charged Fire Extinguisher is mounted in the cab and a First Aid Kit is present.

### Phase 3: Operational & Functionality Tests (Moving)
The final step requires testing the machine's moving parts at low speed in a clear area to confirm the controls respond safely.

1. **Warning Devices**: Test the horn, travel alarms, reversing beepers, and the roof-mounted flashing amber beacon light.
2. **Travel & Steering**: Test forward and reverse tracking, track brakes, and ensuring steering controls respond accurately without pulling.
3. **Hydraulic Controls**: Raise, lower, and cycle the boom, dipper, bucket, and slew ring to check for stuttering, sticking, or pressure drop.
4. **Quick-Hitch Safety Pin**: If using an attachment, physically check that the quick-hitch safety pin or automatic hydraulic locking hook is completely engaged.

## Unalterable WorkSafe Compliance Fields

To make the app legally defensible during a WorkSafe audit, the app must automatically record these four data points behind the scenes:

1. **The Digital Signature**: The operator must physically sign the screen to verify accountability.
2. **GPS & Timestamping**: The app must log the exact date, time, and coordinates when the form was submitted, proving the precheck happened before the shift, not backdated.
3. **The Engine Hour Reading**: Compulsory for aligning the precheck with your service records.
4. **The "No-Go" Action Plan**: If an operator flags a critical fail (e.g., failed seatbelt or oil leak), the app must force them to comment, capture a photo, and automatically flag the machine as grounded.
